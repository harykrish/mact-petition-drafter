# National Insurance Co. Ltd. v. Pranay Sethi, (2017) 16 SCC 680

Constitution Bench. Settled two things the Sarla Verma framework had left
inconsistent: **future prospects** and the **conventional heads**.

## Future prospects (addition to income)

### Salaried with permanent / stable job
| Age band   | Future prospects |
|------------|:----------------:|
| 18 – 40    | +50% |
| 40 – 50    | +30% |
| 50 – 60    | +15% |
| above 60   | nil |

### Self-employed / fixed-wage earner
| Age band   | Future prospects |
|------------|:----------------:|
| 18 – 40    | +40% |
| 40 – 50    | +25% |
| 50 – 60    | +10% |
| above 60   | nil |

> The verifier must pick the row by **both** age band **and** employment type.
> Employment type comes from a financial-stream fact (salary slip / ITR vs.
> business income / wage record).

## Conventional heads (death cases) — base figures
| Head                | Amount (₹) |
|---------------------|:----------:|
| Loss of estate      | 15,000 |
| Loss of consortium  | 40,000 |
| Funeral expenses    | 15,000 |

These base figures **increase by 10% every three years** from the date of the
judgment. Apply the enhancement appropriate to the petition's filing date.

## Injury-case heads (non-pecuniary, supported by documented facts)
For an injured claimant the conventional/non-pecuniary heads typically pleaded,
each only where a KB fact supports it:
- Pain, suffering and trauma
- Loss of amenities / enjoyment of life
- Future medical expenses (from a medical-stream prognosis fact)
- Attendant / nursing charges (where disability requires care)
- Special diet and conveyance during treatment
- Loss of income during the treatment / recovery period (actual)

## What the rubric enforces
- M7: future-prospects addition matches the correct row above.
- S1: applicable conventional/non-pecuniary heads are present where facts exist.
