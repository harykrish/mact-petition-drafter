# Sarla Verma v. DTC, (2009) 6 SCC 121

Foundational Supreme Court authority that standardized the **multiplier method**
for computing loss of dependency / future earnings in motor-accident claims.

## Multiplier table (by age of the victim)
| Age band      | Multiplier |
|---------------|:----------:|
| up to 15      | 15 (notional) |
| 15 – 20       | 18 |
| 21 – 25       | 18 |
| 26 – 30       | 17 |
| 31 – 35       | 16 |
| 36 – 40       | 15 |
| 41 – 45       | 14 |
| 46 – 50       | 13 |
| 51 – 55       | 11 |
| 56 – 60       | 9  |
| 61 – 65       | 7  |
| 66 – 70       | 5  |

> The reconciler/verifier should treat the band boundaries exactly as the
> Tribunal applies them; where the precise boundary is contested, flag rather
> than guess.

## Deduction for personal & living expenses (death/dependency cases)
| Number of dependants | Deduction |
|----------------------|:---------:|
| 2 – 3                | 1/3 |
| 4 – 6                | 1/4 |
| more than 6          | 1/5 |
| bachelor (self)      | 1/2 |

## Core formula (death / dependency)
```
loss of dependency = annual income
                     × (1 + future prospects)        ← per Pranay Sethi
                     × multiplier                     ← table above
                     × (1 − personal-expense deduction)
```

## Injury / permanent-disability adaptation
For a living, injured claimant the "loss of future earning capacity" uses the
same multiplier and future-prospects logic but scales by the **functional
disability percentage** rather than applying a dependency deduction:
```
loss of future earning = annual income
                         × (1 + future prospects)
                         × multiplier
                         × functional disability %
```

This is the formula the petition rubric (M8) re-computes.
